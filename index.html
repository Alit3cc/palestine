Who We Are

Why Solidarity
Matters

Palestine Now is an independent, nonpartisan platform built to educate, connect, and mobilize people of conscience across the world. We believe that justice is universal — when one people is denied their fundamental rights, the silence of the international community is complicity.

Palestinian people have been living under occupation, blockade, and dispossession for over 75 years. The situation has been documented and condemned by the United Nations, the International Court of Justice, Amnesty International, Human Rights Watch, and B'Tselem. This is not a political opinion — it is a documented humanitarian reality.

We do not advocate for any armed group or political party. We advocate for human rights, international law, and the inalienable dignity of every person regardless of their origin.

Solidarity

Solidarity is not charity — it is the recognition that our struggles are connected. When we stand together, we are stronger.

Justice & International Law

UN Resolution 242, the Fourth Geneva Convention, and ICJ rulings all affirm Palestinian rights. We stand on the side of law.

Nonviolent Resistance

We support peaceful, legal forms of resistance: boycott, education, protest, and democratic advocacy. Violence is not our path.

In most democracies, yes. Consumer boycott is a protected form of political expression under freedom of speech and freedom of commerce. In the United States, the First Amendment protects political boycotts. In the European Union, the European Court of Human Rights has repeatedly upheld the right to call for boycotts.

In Germany specifically, the legal situation is nuanced. Calling for a product boycott for political reasons is generally permitted under Meinungsfreiheit (freedom of opinion) — but context matters. Always inform yourself about local laws before organizing public campaigns.

BDS stands for Boycott, Divestment, and Sanctions. It is a Palestinian-led nonviolent campaign launched in 2005, calling on the international community to apply economic and political pressure on Israel until it complies with international law — specifically: ending the occupation of Palestinian territories, granting full equality to Arab-Palestinian citizens of Israel, and honoring the right of return for Palestinian refugees.

The BDS movement draws inspiration from the South African anti-apartheid movement. Learn more at bdsmovement.net.

The UN Security Council, the UN General Assembly, and the International Court of Justice have all issued relevant rulings. UN Security Council Resolution 242 (1967) demands Israeli withdrawal from occupied territories. The ICJ's 2004 Advisory Opinion ruled Israel's West Bank barrier illegal under international law. In 2024, the ICJ issued interim measures in a genocide case brought by South Africa, and separately ruled that Israel's occupation of Palestinian territories is unlawful.

The Fourth Geneva Convention prohibits the transfer of civilian populations into occupied territories (i.e., settlements) and mandates protection of civilian life. Amnesty International and Human Rights Watch have both published detailed findings of apartheid conditions and war crimes.

The occupation of the West Bank, Gaza Strip, and East Jerusalem is a legal fact under international law, not a matter of political opinion. The International Court of Justice issued an advisory opinion in July 2024 declaring Israel's continued presence in the Occupied Palestinian Territory unlawful.

Over 140 UN member states recognize Palestine as a state. The UN has passed hundreds of resolutions calling for an end to the occupation. What remains "contested" is how, not whether, the occupation should end.

This is a solidarity platform — not a neutral news outlet. Solidarity means taking a position. We take the position that international law, human rights, and the protection of civilian life are non-negotiable. We do not equate occupier and occupied, oppressor and oppressed.

This does not mean we are indifferent to Israeli civilian suffering — we condemn attacks on all civilians. But false balance is not objectivity: when one side controls the land, the borders, the water, the air, and the economy of another people, "both sides" framing obscures reality.
